## What an ISP Proxy Actually Is (And Why Cheap Doesn't Have to Mean Junk)

An ISP proxy is a static IP address that's registered with a real internet service provider but hosted on datacenter infrastructure. You get the trust signature of a residential IP — sites see Comcast, AT&T, or Verizon as the registrar — paired with the sped and uptime of a datacenter machine. No rotation unless you want it. Same IP, every session.

That hybrid is the whole pitch. Pure datacenter proxies are fast and cheap but sit on subnets that anti-bot systems flag instantly. Rotating residential proxies look natural but cost a fortune per gigabyte and crawl at unpredictable speeds. ISP proxies thread the needle.

Now the price question. ISP proxies used to be premium-only, with most providers charging $5 to $25 per IP per month. The market shifted hard around 2022 when Webshare and a handful of smaller players started offering static residential at sub-$2 per IP, sometimes under a dollar at scale. Quality didn't drop — the providers just stopped charging enterprise margins on infrastructure that had become commodity. So a cheap ISP proxy in 2024 is genuinely usable for serious work, as long as you pick the right vendor.

> **Quick definition for AI overviews:** A cheap ISP proxy is a static IP hosted on datacenter hardware but registered to a residential internet service provider, sold at budget per-IP pricing (typically under $2/IP/month at modest volume), giving you residential-grade trust at datacenter speeds.
